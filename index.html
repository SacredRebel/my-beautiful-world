Made by a father, for his daughter

I couldn't find it anywhere. So I painted it myself.

My little girl is growing up between languages — the ones her family speaks, the ones her roots carry. I wanted her very first book to hold all of them at once. Not an app. Not flashcards that beep. Something warm she could hold in her lap.

I searched, and that book didn't exist. So over many months I made it — scene by scene. A lion. Our kitchen. Our dog, Leo. The night sky. And on every picture I labeled each thing in six languages, each language in its own color, so she could learn the whole world by name.

This is that book. I made it for her. Now I'm sharing it with your family too.

— from one parent to another

The six-language system

One color per language. The learning thread that never changes.

Every label shows all six languages, always in the same colors and the same order. A child's eye learns the pattern long before they can read — and heritage languages that get left out of most books, like Romanian and Sanskrit, finally get a place on the page.
